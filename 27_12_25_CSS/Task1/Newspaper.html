INDIA STEPS UP ACTION ON COUGH SYRUP SAFETY AFTER GLOBAL ALERTS

Indian health authorities have intensified inspections and quality checks on cough syrups following international concerns over contaminated medicines linked to serious health risks. Regulatory bodies have ordered stricter compliance with manufacturing standards and increased testing of pharmaceutical exports to ensure patient safety. The move comes after reports from multiple countries raised alarms about the presence of toxic substances in certain syrup formulations. Officials have clarified that only specific batches were affected, and action has been taken against manufacturers found violating quality norms. Health experts have advised consumers to use medicines only as prescribed by registered doctors and to avoid self-medication, especially in the case of children. GOVERNMENT ORDERS NATIONWIDE AUDIT OF PHARMA UNITS

In response to growing concerns, the central government has announced a nationwide audit of pharmaceutical manufacturing units producing liquid oral medicines, including cough syrups. The audit aims to identify gaps in quality assurance, raw material sourcing, and storage practices. Officials stated that corrective measures will be enforced strictly, and non-compliant units may face permanent shutdowns. Industry bodies have welcomed the move, stating that maintaining global trust in Indian pharmaceuticals is critical for public health and export credibility.
